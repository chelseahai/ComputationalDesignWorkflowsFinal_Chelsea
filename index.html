Temporal Structure

Temporal Climate Heatmap

This temporal heatmap structure visualizes climate data as a color-coded grid where each cell represents a year and climate variable. Time flows horizontally from 1880 to 2023, with three climate indicators stacked vertically. Color intensity represents the magnitude of each measurement - darker reds show higher temperatures, deeper blues indicate higher CO2 levels and sea level rise. The structure reveals temporal patterns and trends that emerge when viewing climate data as a spatial-temporal matrix rather than traditional line graphs.

Relational Structure

Social Network Analysis

This relational structure visualizes connections between people, organizations, and topics in a creative technology ecosystem. Nodes represent individuals, institutions, and subject areas, while edges show relationships like collaboration, expertise, and partnerships. The network reveals how knowledge flows between different domains and how communities form around shared interests.

Geospatial Structure

Leading Pedestrian Intervals

This geospatial visualization shows Leading Pedestrian Intervals (LPI) across New York City. LPIs give pedestrians a head start at traffic signals, improving safety and walkability. The map reveals patterns in pedestrian infrastructure investment and identifies areas where walkability enhancements have been prioritized.
